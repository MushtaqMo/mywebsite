All about Perfume

The History of Perfume

Perfume has been around since ancient times. Perfume has been said to have been discovered by early Egyptians from 3000 BC to 100 BC. To create scents, egyptians in acient times often used lotus flower, water lillies, roses and cinnemon. To create their perfume they would soak the plants in oil to create a concentrated perfume. Unfortunately, only the rich could afford to have the luxury to own perfume. Luckily, in modern times there are many cheap, yet good quality perfumes for people of all economical class backgrounds to use and enjoy.

Perfumes across different cultures

Perfume is widely used in the Middle East and is heavily ingrained in their culture. Men and women alike, use perfume on a daily basis. With the popularity of oud in the Middle East, people often favour strong, powerful scents that make a statement. In China woody scents are preffered in dry climates, whereas fruity florals are popular in warmer areas. In the west, people often favour lighter, citrus or floral based scents. The most popular scents in the UK include, rose, lily of the valley and violet.
